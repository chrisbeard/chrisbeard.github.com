About Me

I'm a junior at Binghamton University, where I study Computer Science and Mathematics.

Lately, I've been interested in hackathons, trying new ideas, and slacklining.

I'm working on a couple of projects which will soon benefit students at Binghamton University - stay tuned.

I spent my summer at the Air Force Research Laboratory in Rome, N.Y., where I did research on active ranking and saw a ton of top-notch research talks. Ask me about my work; I'd love to talk about it!

If you want to get in touch, e-mail is the best way.

Music

I've been playing trombone since 2003, and I currently study with Don Robertson at Binghamton University.

I am a member of Don's low brass studio ensemble, as well as the Binghamton University Symphony Orchestra, which is directed by Dr. Timothy Perry.

You might also see me play in some of the music department's recitals, or at Binghamton's commencement ceremonies.

I make multi-track recordings of myself on trombone, and compose alternative electronic music. Some of my work is available on soundcloud.

Programming

I worked on a research project with Kevin Johnson, where made initial steps towards creating secure memory compartments on the OpenRISC 1200 CPU.

I've been hacking! I had a great time at HackPrinceton and HackBU last semester, and look forward to future hackathons. You can check out my hacks on my challengepost page.

I'm working with Itai to develop seemeco.de, a site which helps programmers build up a GitHub portfolio. We started it at HackPrinceton, where it won Wolfram Alpha's Best Education Hack.

Speaking of GitHub, you can find mine here.

Lately, I've been programming in C++, but I use C and Python from time to time. I used to program a lot in Java, and look forward to getting back into it at some point.
